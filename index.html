Departure Guide

Day Before: Sunday, April 26

Online Check-In Opens: Sunday, April 26 at 11:30 AM

Check-in opens exactly 24 hours before departure. Since United operates this flight, check in on united.com or the United app. Look up your reservation in the United app under My Trips, or use the confirmation number from your ANA booking email.

What you'll need during check-in:

  • Passport details (number, expiration date, issuing country)
  • Your hotel address in Japan — required for APIS (Advance Passenger Information). Have this ready before you start.
  • Emergency contact name & phone number
  • Seat confirmation — review/change your seat assignment
  • Dangerous goods acknowledgment (standard checkbox)

Complete APIS data is mandatory — you cannot get a boarding pass without it. Don't skip or rush through the passport info fields.

Boarding Pass: You Need a Paper Copy

United does not support mobile boarding passes for international departures from the US. This applies to all passengers on this flight, regardless of the codeshare.

What to do: After completing online check-in on united.com, you'll be offered a PDF to print. Print it at home Sunday evening. This is your boarding pass.

If you can't print at home: No problem. Use any self-service kiosk at Terminal C (Level 2, Zones E–G) to reprint. Scan your passport at the kiosk and it will pull up your reservation and print a boarding pass.

Note: Since this is ANA ticket stock operated by United, the United app may show limited functionality. If the app doesn't pull up your booking, don't worry — the kiosk at the airport will work with your passport.

Pre-Flight Checklist

  • 11:30 AM Sunday — Online check-in opens on united.com. Do this immediately. Have your passport and Japan hotel address ready. Print your boarding pass at home (PDF).
  • Passport check — Confirm it's valid through at least October 2026. Japan requires validity for the duration of your stay. US citizens do not need a visa for stays under 90 days.
  • Visit Japan Web — Pre-register your customs and immigration info at vjw.digital.go.jp. This speeds up arrival at Narita significantly.
  • Print boarding pass — After online check-in, print the PDF. Mobile boarding passes are not accepted for this international departure. Bring the paper copy.
  • Pack carry-on — 1 bag (max 22 x 14 x 9 in) + 1 personal item. Keep under 22 lbs total if possible (ANA's limit, loosely enforced on United metal). Pack everything you need: passport, phone charger, headphones, snacks, change of clothes. No checked bag means nothing to lose — and a faster exit at Narita.
  • Japan medication check — Critical Japan strictly prohibits: Adderall/amphetamine ADHD meds, pseudoephedrine (Sudafed), some codeine products, and ALL cannabis/CBD products. If you take any prescription medications, verify they're legal in Japan before packing them.
  • Confirm flight — Check united.com or the United app for schedule changes. Your e-ticket advises reconfirming 24–72 hours before departure.
  • Set alarm — 5:00 AM. Set a backup alarm.
  • Charge devices — Phone, tablet, portable battery. The 787 has power outlets at every seat, but start charged.

At the Airport: Step by Step

Step 1 — Enter Terminal C

After curbside drop-off on the upper level (departures), walk through the doors into the Terminal C check-in hall.

You're now on Level 2 — economy check-in.

Step 2 — Check-In & Document Verification

No checked bag You're traveling carry-on only, so this step is quick. No bag drop needed.

If you checked in online and printed your boarding pass: Head to a self-service kiosk in Zones E–G on Level 2. Scan your passport for identity and APIS verification. The kiosk confirms your boarding pass. You're done — head straight to security.

If you did NOT check in online: The kiosk handles everything — check-in, passport verification, and boarding pass printing. Scan your passport (easiest) or enter the confirmation number from your ANA booking email. Allow 15–20 minutes.

Codeshare note You check in at United counters/kiosks, not ANA. Your ANA confirmation (in your booking email or United app → My Trips) and ANA ticket stock work at United kiosks.

Step 3 — Security (TSA PreCheck)

Head up to the Mezzanine level. Follow signs for Security / All Gates.

The main checkpoint has dedicated TSA PreCheck lanes. Monday mid-morning PreCheck wait: typically 5–15 minutes.

Have in hand: Passport + printed boarding pass. TSA PreCheck doesn't change what documents you need — it just speeds up the screening.

PreCheck rules: Leave shoes on. Laptop stays in bag. No liquids removal. Belt stays on. Walk through the metal detector (not the body scanner). Fast and simple.

TSA PreCheck You have PreCheck. Use the dedicated lane. It's marked clearly.

After security: You can stow your passport for now. You won't need it again until the boarding gate. Fill your water bottle at a refill station (there's one near Gate C121).

Step 4 — Walk to Your Gate (Concourse C3)

After security, you're airside. All three concourses (C1, C2, C3) are connected — no additional security needed.

International wide-body flights to NRT depart from Concourse C3, gates C120–C139. This is a 10–15 minute walk from the main security checkpoint. Head left/straight and follow signs for gates C120+.

You'll pass restrooms, water refill stations, and food options on the walk. There are additional restrooms near the C130s gates.

Gate changes are common at EWR. Before committing to a long walk, check your gate on the departure board right after security and on the United app (Flight Status section — the boarding pass view can show stale info). Check again if you stop for food. Don't camp out far from the gate area until you've confirmed the gate number.

Step 5 — At the Gate

You should arrive at the gate area by 10:15 AM — 75 minutes before departure.

Boarding begins ~10:40 AM (about 50 minutes before departure for international 787 flights).

At the gate, have ready: Passport + printed boarding pass. The gate agent will re-check your passport when you board — this is standard for all international departures. They scan both documents.

Boarding order (United's system):

  1. Pre-boarding (disabilities, families with small children, military)
  2. Group 1 — Business/first class, Premier Platinum/Gold, Star Alliance Gold
  3. Group 2 — Premier Silver, Star Alliance Silver, United cardholders
  4. Group 3 — Economy window seats
  5. Group 4 — Economy middle seats
  6. Group 5 — Economy aisle seats

As an ANA-ticketed economy passenger, you'll be in Group 3, 4, or 5 based on your seat position. Tip: A window seat puts you in Group 3 (earliest economy boarding), giving you first access to overhead bin space.

Doors close at ~11:20 AM. Be at the gate before boarding starts.

What If...?

Every question you might have, answered in advance.

"What if I miss the Terminal C exit on the airport road?"
You cannot get lost. The airport road is a loop. If you pass Terminal C, just keep driving — the road will loop you back around to the terminals again. It adds about 5–10 minutes. No U-turns needed, no backing up. Just keep going forward.
"What if I accidentally go to the wrong level (Arrivals instead of Departures)?"
Not a problem. Stephen can still get out and walk into the building — there are elevators and escalators inside that go between levels. It's not ideal, but it works. Just pull over wherever it's safe and let him out.
"What if a traffic officer tells me to move?"
This is normal. They're managing the flow of cars at the curb. It doesn't mean you're in trouble. Just give a wave, finish up quickly, and pull away when you're ready. Stephen only has a carry-on, so the stop is quick.
"What if I take the wrong lanes on the Turnpike (Express instead of Local)?"
If you accidentally take the Express/Western lanes, you won't be able to exit at 13A. Don't panic. Take Exit 14 (which is available from both spurs) and follow signs for Newark Airport from there. You can also get off at an earlier Express exit and navigate via local roads. The important thing: don't try to cross between the lane sections — just take the next available exit.
"What if I end up on the Turnpike by accident after the drop-off?"
That's actually exactly where you want to be! You need the Turnpike South to get home. Just make sure you're heading South (toward Camden / Philadelphia), not North (toward New York). The signs will be clear.
"What if traffic is really bad and we're running late?"
The timeline has buffer built in. Even if you arrive at 9:30 AM instead of 9:00 AM, Stephen still has 2 hours before the flight. Don't speed or stress — safety is more important than minutes. If things are truly dire, Stephen can call United at 1-800-864-8331 from the car.
"What if I need to stop or pull over after the drop-off?"
Once you're past the terminal, there are safe places to pull over on the airport access roads. The Cell Phone Waiting Lot is free and accessible from the airport road — follow signs. It's a calm, quiet parking area. You can stop there, collect yourself, set up your GPS for home, and leave when you're ready.
